The Loving Atmosphere

Paris The City Of Love is the capital city of an European country named France. It is called the city of love because of the romantic atmosphere, it is not just a nickname but a perfect description for all the romantic vibes they find there.

Population

Paris is the capital and most populous city of France. It has an area of 105 square kilometres and A fun fact is that the city alone has a population of about 2,229,621 within its administrative limits. The city is both a commune and department and forms the centre and headquarters of the Paris Region.

Lights

The beautiful city does not only have one nickname but it is also called the city of lights (La Ville Lumiere). It is the first European city to usse gas street lighting on a grand scale on its boulevards.

Know Your City

Let us take you on an amazing journey to the ever busy streets of Paris that overflow with Culture, Art, Beauty, and History.

Tracks

Paris is a major railway, highway, and air-transport hub served by two international airports: Paris–Charles de Gaulle (Europe's second busiest airport) and Paris–Orly. The Paris Métro, which opened in 1900, serves 5.23 million passengers daily, making it Europe's second busiest metro system after the Moscow Metro. With 262 million passengers, Gare du Nord is the world's 24th busiest railway station, but the first outside of Japan.

History

Paris receives about 38 million visitors annually, measured by hotel stays. It is ranked as the second most visited travel destination in the world after Bangkok. Tourism to Paris and its region fell dramatically to 17.5 million visitors due the current situation of COVID 19. The city also had the privilege to host the Olympic Games in 1900, 1924 and will host the 2024 Summer Olympics.

Side

By the end of the 12th century, Paris had become the political, economic, religious, and cultural capital of France. The royal residence, was located on the western extremity of the city. Maurice de Sully, bishop of Paris, began building of the Notre Dame Cathedral at its eastern extremity in 1163, during the reign of Louis VII.

The North Atlantic Current influences Paris, which has a typical Western European oceanic climate . The weather is pleasant and mildly rainy throughout the year. Summer days are typically warm and pleasant, with average temperatures and plenty of sunshine.  There are a few days each year when the temperature exceeds 32 degrees Celsius (90 degrees Fahrenheit). Longer periods of more intense heat can occur, such as during the 2003 heat wave, when temperatures consistently above 30 °C (86 °F) for weeks, reached 40 °C (104 °F) on certain days, and seldom cooled down at night. The days are milder and the nights are cooler in the spring and autumn.
